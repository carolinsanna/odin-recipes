Odin Recipes

The Vikings, a Nordic people who lived during the Viking Age (8th to 11th centuries), had a diet rich in natural ingredients from both land and sea. Their meals were designed to provide the energy needed for their daily activities.

Main Components of the Viking Diet:

  • Meat:
    • They consumed pork, beef, and poultry, prepared in various ways such as roasting, boiling, or smoking.
  • Fish and Seafood:
    • As skilled sailors, they had access to salmon, cod, herring, and shellfish, which were eaten fresh, salted, or smoked.
  • Cereals:
    • They cultivated barley and oats to make bread, porridge, and beer. Barley bread was a staple in their diet.
  • Vegetables:
    • Their diet included cabbage, onions, garlic, carrots, and turnips, used in soups, stews, and as side dishes.
  • Fruits and Nuts:
    • They consumed apples, pears, berries, and nuts, both fresh and dried, and used them in desserts and beverages.
